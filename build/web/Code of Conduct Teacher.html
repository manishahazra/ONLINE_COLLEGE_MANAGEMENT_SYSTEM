Code of Conduct Teacher


Centered and Justified Text

Code of Conduct serves as a guiding compass for teachers seek to steer an ethical and respectful course through their career in teaching and to uphold the honour and dignity of the teaching profession. Teachers uphold human dignity and promote equality and emotional and cognitive development. A teacher's code of conduct is a set of ethical standards that guide teachers' behavior and decision-making. It helps teachers maintain professional integrity, uphold the dignity of the teaching profession, and ensure the well-being of students. A teacher's code of conduct may include: • Prioritizing students Teachers should put the welfare of their students first and demonstrate care, fairness, and empathy. • Maintaining professional relationships Teachers should maintain professional relationships with students, colleagues, and parents. • Upholding high standards Teachers should uphold the highest standards of personal conduct and integrity. • Respecting diversity Teachers should respect the diversity of their students, including their spiritual and cultural values, social justice, freedom, democracy, and the environment. • Avoiding inappropriate relationships Teachers should not engage in inappropriate relationships with students. • Maintaining professional growth Teachers should continuously seek professional growth through study, research, and active membership in professional organizations. • Discouraging plagiarism Teachers should discourage or avoid plagiarism and other non-ethical behaviors. • Participating in extracurricular activities Teachers should participate in extracurricular activities, including community service.
