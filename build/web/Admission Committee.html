Admission Committee


Centered and Justified Text

An admission committee is a group of people who evaluate applicants to determine if they are a good fit for a college. The committee's responsibilities include: • Establishing admission criteria: The committee sets and enforces admission criteria for all courses offered by the college. • Reviewing applications: The committee reviews applications to confirm eligibility. • Identifying qualified candidates: The committee identifies and approves qualified candidates for admission. • Creating a merit list: The committee creates a merit list of admitted students. • Counseling students: The committee provides counseling to students and parents on the college programs. • Maintaining student files: The committee keeps student personal files with the necessary certificates. • Improving admission policies: The committee analyzes changes to the college admission policies to improve the process. • Considering diversity: The committee aims to increase the number of qualified students from diverse backgrounds. • Accommodating students with disabilities: The committee provides guidelines for accommodating students with disabilities. An admission committee is typically made up of admissions officers, faculty members, academic deans, and sometimes current students.
