Finance Committee


Centered and Justified Text

The purpose of the Finance Committee (the “Committee”) is to assist the Board of Directors in fulfilling its oversight responsibilities with respect to the monitoring and oversight of the Corporation's financial resources, including its capital management, recovery and resolution planning processes, and asset/liability ... A finance committee is a group that monitors and oversees an organization's finances. The committee's responsibilities include: • Financial planning Developing an annual budget, setting long-term financial goals, and monitoring adherence to the budget • Financial reporting Creating financial reports • Internal controls Creating, approving, and updating policies to protect the organization's assets • Fixed assets and stock Ensuring the organization maintains proper control of its fixed assets, materials, and stock • Financial systems Ensuring the organization's financial systems enable accountability and transparency, produce an effective audit trail, and include a segregation of duties A finance committee is usually a standing committee of the board of directors. The board chairman serves as the chair of the finance committee and is responsible for ensuring the committee does its job.
