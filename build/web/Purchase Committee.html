Purchase Committee


Centered and Justified Text

A purchase committee is a group of people who evaluate and review purchasing documentation to recommend the best supplier or service provider. They are responsible for ensuring that procurement practices are consistent and correct. Here are some of the responsibilities of a purchase committee: • Reviewing documentation The committee reviews purchasing documentation to recommend the best supplier or service provider. • Monitoring and verifying The committee monitors and verifies that approved procurement procedures are being followed. • Preparing guidelines The committee prepares guidelines and systems for procuring materials, equipment, and other items. • Coordinating purchases The committee coordinates purchases across departments to ensure that required items are procured on time. • Negotiating with suppliers The committee negotiates with suppliers to ensure that the best quality items are procured at competitive prices. • Scrutinizing requisitions The committee scrutinizes requisitions for equipment and decides whether to purchase it or manufacture it in-house. • Calling and scrutinizing tenders The committee calls and scrutinizes tenders and quotations for items to be purchased. The head of the organization establishes and manages the purchase committee and acts as a non-voting member at all meetings.
