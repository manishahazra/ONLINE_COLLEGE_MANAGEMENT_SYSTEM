Principal and Vice Principal

Principal


Centered and Justified Text

A college principal is the head of a college, responsible for its day-to-day management and academic leadership: • Responsibilities The principal is responsible for ensuring the college runs smoothly and in line with its mission and vision. • Appointment The principal is appointed by the college's Board of Governors. • Term The principal's term is usually five years, but may be extendable for another five years. • Role in the Commonwealth In some Commonwealth countries, the principal is both the chief executive and the chief academic officer of the college or university.

Vice Principal

A college vice principal is a senior-level administrator who assists the principal in the day-to-day management of the college: • Academic responsibilities Collaborates with the principal to implement the college's vision, mission, and goals. Oversees academic activities, including planning and implementing the academic timetable. • Non-academic responsibilities Oversees non-curriculum areas, including managing resources and leading non-teaching staff. • Other responsibilities Acts as the acting principal in the principal's absence. Maintains effective data management systems, such as student records and attendance. Provides general assistance to students. The vice principal's authority is generally less than the principal's, though they have similar authority among students. The term "vice principal" has different meanings in different parts of the world: • British English and Canadian English: A senior person at a college or university • North American English: A senior teacher at a College. .
